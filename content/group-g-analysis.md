# Group G Analysis — World Cup 2026

## 🏟️ Group G: Belgium 🇧🇪 | Egypt 🇪🇬 | Iran 🇮🇷 | New Zealand 🇳🇿

### Overview
Belgium's "Golden Generation" has one last shot at glory, but the window is closing fast. They're clear favorites in a group that features Egypt's growing ambition, Iran's Asian consistency, and New Zealand's underdog energy. All matches are on the West Coast (LA, Seattle, Vancouver) — expect passionate atmospheres from the large diaspora communities in North America.

---

### 🇧🇪 Belgium — The Last Dance
**FIFA Ranking:** 8
**World Cup Pedigree:** 15th appearance — 3rd place in 2018
**Key Strength:** Still loaded with talent across Europe's top clubs; tournament experience; tactical versatility
**Key Weakness:** The golden generation (De Bruyne, Lukaku, Courtois) is aging — is the next wave ready? Team chemistry questions after disappointing 2022 (group stage exit vs Morocco/Croatia)
**Key Players:** De Bruyne (likely final World Cup), Doku, Openda — mix of experience and explosive youth
**Verdict:** Belgium should top this group, but they're not the force they were in 2018. A disastrous 2022 campaign (lost to Morocco, drew with Croatia, eliminated in groups) raised serious questions. If De Bruyne is fit and firing, they're dangerous. If not, this group could bite them.

### 🇪🇬 Egypt — Mo Salah's World Cup
**FIFA Ranking:** 34
**World Cup Pedigree:** 4th appearance (first since 2018)
**Key Strength:** Mo Salah — one of the greatest players of his generation; organized defense; AFCON pedigree (7x champions)
**Key Weakness:** Over-reliance on Salah; limited depth compared to top nations; historically poor World Cup record
**Historic Moment:** Reached 2018 World Cup after 28-year absence, but lost all three group games
**Verdict:** Everything revolves around Salah. If he's in form, Egypt can beat anyone on their day. They'll target 2nd place and could get it if Iran and New Zealand can take points off each other. The Egypt-Iran match could be the group decider.

### 🇮🇷 Iran — Asia's Dark Horse
**FIFA Ranking:** 20-25 range
**World Cup Pedigree:** 7th appearance
**Key Strength:** Defensively resilient, physically strong, organized — hard to break down. Experienced at World Cups.
**Key Weakness:** Limited attacking threat; tend to sit deep and hope for counters/set pieces; can struggle to create against packed defenses
**Historic Moment:** Beat Wales 2-0 in Qatar 2022; consistently qualify from Asia's tough confederation
**Verdict:** Iran are the classic "nobody wants to play them" team. They won't dominate possession but they'll make every game a grind. Could easily nick 2nd place if Egypt underperform. The Iran-Egypt clash will be intense.

### 🇳🇿 New Zealand — The All Whites
**FIFA Ranking:** 90-100 range
**World Cup Pedigree:** 3rd appearance (first since 2010)
**Key Strength:** Physical, competitive, improving quality through Chris Wood and European-based players; OFC dominance
**Key Weakness:** Massive quality gap compared to the other three teams; limited experience at the highest level
**Historic Moment:** Went unbeaten at 2010 World Cup (3 draws, including vs Italy!) — the only unbeaten team that tournament
**Verdict:** Huge underdogs but with a romantic story. The 2010 side proved they can compete — drew with Italy, Paraguay, and Slovakia. They'll aim to be hard to beat and steal a point or two. Finishing 4th is the realistic expectation, but a shock draw isn't impossible.
