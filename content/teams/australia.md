# Australia 🇦🇺 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** ~30
- **Confederation:** AFC
- **World Cup Appearances:** 7
- **Best Result:** Round of 16 (2022)
- **Group:** D (vs United States, Paraguay, UEFA Playoff C winner)

## The Story
The Socceroos shocked the world at Qatar 2022 — beating Denmark, drawing with Tunisia, and reaching the Round of 16 for the first time since 2006. That tournament proved Australia are no longer a "make up the numbers" team. They compete, they fight, and they can beat good sides.

Group D alongside the USA is tough, but Australia's trajectory is upward. More players than ever are in European leagues, the domestic A-League is improving, and the 2022 experience built genuine belief.

## Playing Style
- Physical intensity — one of the most athletic squads in the tournament
- Never-quit mentality — the Aussie spirit runs through everything
- Organized defensive shape with quick counter-attacks
- Improving technical quality through European-based players
- Set-piece threat — tall, strong, dangerous from corners

## Key Players to Watch
- A growing pool of Premier League, Championship, and European talent
- Experienced defensive spine with World Cup knowhow from 2022
- Young attackers emerging through European academies

## Strengths
✅ 2022 Round of 16 — momentum and belief from Qatar
✅ Physical and athletic — never outworked
✅ Improving quality through European player development
✅ Nothing-to-lose mentality — classic Aussie underdog energy
✅ Beat Denmark at 2022 WC — big-game credentials

## Weaknesses
❌ USA with home advantage and Paraguay's CONMEBOL grit — tough group
❌ Limited individual star power compared to top 20 teams
❌ Can struggle against highly technical opponents
❌ AFC qualifying doesn't fully prepare for World Cup intensity
❌ Depth beyond starting XI drops significantly

## 💰 Betting Outlook
- **To qualify (top 2):** Tough ~2.80 — battling Paraguay for 2nd
- **Australia vs Paraguay — Draw:** The key match. Physical, tight, 1-1.
- **Australia to beat UEFA Playoff C winner:** Should be bankable regardless of opponent.
