# Croatia 🇭🇷 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 10
- **Confederation:** UEFA
- **World Cup Appearances:** 7
- **Best Result:** Finalists (2018)
- **Group:** L (vs England, Ghana, Panama)
- **Key Question:** Can the post-Modrić generation maintain Croatia's insane overperformance?

## The Story
Croatia are the greatest overachievers in football history. A nation of 4 million people has reached two of the last three World Cup semi-finals (finalists in 2018, semi-finalists in 2022). Luka Modrić — one of the greatest midfielders ever — has driven this golden era, but father time is undefeated. The 2026 World Cup likely marks the transition to the next generation.

The question is enormous: can Gvardiol, Šučić, and Baturina replicate what Modrić, Rakitić, and Perišić achieved? Croatia's system breeds midfield technicians like nowhere else. If the culture holds, they'll compete. If it doesn't, this could be a painful tournament.

## Playing Style
- Midfield mastery — control, technique, intelligence
- Patient build-up with sudden acceleration through the middle
- Resilient and mentally tough — never beaten until the final whistle
- Set-piece threat — experienced, well-drilled from dead balls
- Counter-pressing — win the ball back quickly in dangerous areas

## Key Players to Watch
- **Joško Gvardiol** — Man City's ball-playing CB. Already world-class at 24. The new face of Croatia.
- **Luka Šučić** — The midfield heir. Technical, composed, Modrić-esque qualities.
- **Luka Modrić** — If selected at 40/41, the farewell. Even in limited minutes, his presence matters.
- **Mateo Kovačić** — Man City's metronome. Experience and class in equal measure.
- **Andrej Kramarić** — Proven goalscorer, big-game experience.

## Strengths
✅ World Cup DNA — finalists 2018, semi-finalists 2022
✅ Gvardiol is a generational defender
✅ Midfield production line ensures continuity
✅ Mental toughness — Croatia win penalty shootouts and grind knockout games
✅ Always underrated — the market consistently underprices them

## Weaknesses
❌ Post-Modrić transition is the biggest uncertainty
❌ Squad depth beyond starting XI isn't what it was
❌ Aging generation (Modrić, Perišić, Brozović) may be gone or limited
❌ Group L is tough — England immediately, then Ghana's physicality
❌ Lack of a world-class striker — goals come from midfield

## World Cup History Highlights
- 🥉 1998: Third place on debut — Šuker Golden Boot (6 goals)
- 🥈 2018: Finalists — beat England in semis, lost to France in the final
- 🥉 2022: Semi-finalists — beat Brazil on penalties, 3rd place
- 📊 Record: Semi-finals or better in 3 of 7 World Cups — absurd hit rate
