# Austria 🇦🇹 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 24
- **Confederation:** UEFA
- **World Cup Appearances:** 8 (first since 1998 — 28-year gap!)
- **Best Result:** Third place (1954)
- **Group:** J (vs Argentina, Algeria, Jordan)
- **Key Question:** Can Rangnick's pressing revolution translate to World Cup success?

## The Story
Austria are Europe's most improved team. Ralf Rangnick's appointment transformed them from perennial underachievers into a genuine force — aggressive pressing, disciplined structure, and a squad punching well above its weight. Euro 2024 was the coming-out party: they beat Poland, pushed France and the Netherlands, and earned widespread respect.

Returning to the World Cup after 28 years, Austria face defending champions Argentina in Group J. It's a daunting draw, but Rangnick's system is designed to disrupt elite teams. Algeria and Jordan are beatable — 2nd place is the realistic target.

## Playing Style
- Ralf Rangnick's gegenpressing — intense, structured, relentless pressing
- Quick vertical transitions after winning the ball
- Team over individuals — the system IS the star
- Physical and aerially dominant — Bundesliga/Austrian league physicality
- Disciplined defensive shape when absorbing pressure

## Key Players to Watch
- **Marcel Sabitzer** — Borussia Dortmund's experienced midfielder. Goals, assists, leadership.
- **Konrad Laimer** — Bayern Munich's pressing machine. Energy personified.
- **Christoph Baumgartner** — RB Leipzig's versatile attacker. Goals from midfield.
- **Marko Arnautovic** — Veteran striker. Physical, experienced, big personality.
- **Patrick Pentz** — Reliable goalkeeper, improving with experience.

## Strengths
✅ Rangnick's system turns good players into a great team
✅ Euro 2024 proved they can compete with Europe's best
✅ 28-year World Cup absence means zero expectations — pure motivation
✅ Bundesliga backbone — players accustomed to high-intensity football
✅ Collective mentality — no egos, everyone works for the team

## Weaknesses
❌ Argentina in the group — defending champions are a different level
❌ No individual superstars — reliance on the system
❌ World Cup inexperience — nobody in the squad has played a World Cup
❌ Can tire in the second half if pressing intensity drops
❌ Limited attacking creativity against deep blocks
