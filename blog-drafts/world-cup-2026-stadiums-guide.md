# The Ultimate Guide to World Cup 2026 Stadiums — 16 Venues Across 3 Countries

*From the Azteca's altitude to MetLife's skyline views, here's every stadium hosting the biggest World Cup ever.*

---

## A Tournament Like No Other

For the first time in history, three nations — the **United States**, **Mexico**, and **Canada** — will co-host the FIFA World Cup. Sixteen stadiums across 16 cities will host 104 matches over 39 days. It's the most ambitious sporting event ever organized.

Here's your complete guide to every venue.

---

## 🇺🇸 United States (11 Venues)

### MetLife Stadium — East Rutherford, New Jersey
- **Capacity:** ~82,500
- **Notable:** Hosts the **World Cup Final**. Minutes from Manhattan, this is the crown jewel of the tournament.
- **Fun fact:** The only stadium in the world that's home to two NFL teams (Giants & Jets).

### AT&T Stadium — Arlington, Texas
- **Capacity:** ~80,000 (expandable to 100,000)
- **Notable:** Jerry Jones' palace. The retractable roof and massive video screen make this a spectacle venue.
- **Fun fact:** The screen is so large that punted footballs have hit it.

### SoFi Stadium — Inglewood, California
- **Capacity:** ~70,000
- **Notable:** LA's newest mega-venue. Hosts a **semifinal**. The futuristic design and Hollywood backdrop make it iconic.
- **Fun fact:** Cost $5.5 billion to build — the most expensive stadium ever constructed.

### NRG Stadium — Houston, Texas
- **Capacity:** ~72,000
- **Notable:** Retractable roof keeps the brutal Houston heat at bay. A smart choice for summer football.

### Mercedes-Benz Stadium — Atlanta, Georgia
- **Capacity:** ~71,000
- **Notable:** The pinwheel retractable roof is an architectural marvel. Atlanta's passionate soccer culture (thanks to Atlanta United) guarantees electric atmospheres.

### Hard Rock Stadium — Miami, Florida
- **Capacity:** ~65,000
- **Notable:** Hosts a **semifinal**. Miami's Latin American community guarantees incredible energy for South American and Central American matches.

### Lincoln Financial Field — Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
- **Capacity:** ~69,000
- **Notable:** Philly fans are legendarily intense. Visiting teams beware.

### Lumen Field — Seattle, Washington
- **Capacity:** ~69,000
- **Notable:** One of the loudest stadiums in North America. Seattle's deep soccer culture (Sounders) makes this a perfect host.

### Levi's Stadium — Santa Clara, California
- **Capacity:** ~68,500
- **Notable:** Silicon Valley's stadium. Bay Area weather in June/July is ideal for football.

### Gillette Stadium — Foxborough, Massachusetts
- **Capacity:** ~65,000
- **Notable:** New England's home. Close to Boston with strong infrastructure for international visitors.

## 🇲🇽 Mexico (3 Venues)

### Estadio Azteca — Mexico City
- **Capacity:** ~87,000
- **Notable:** The only stadium to host **three** World Cup Finals (1970, 1986, and now matches in 2026). At 2,200m altitude, visiting teams struggle with the thin air. This is sacred ground.
- **Fun fact:** Maradona's "Hand of God" and "Goal of the Century" both happened here in 1986.

### Estadio BBVA — Monterrey
- **Capacity:** ~53,000
- **Notable:** A modern gem backed by the dramatic Sierra Madre mountains. Monterrey's intense football culture rivals anyone's.

### Estadio Akron — Guadalajara
- **Capacity:** ~49,000
- **Notable:** Home of Chivas, one of Mexico's most beloved clubs. The volcanic landscape surrounding the city is stunning.

## 🇨🇦 Canada (2 Venues)

### BMO Field — Toronto
- **Capacity:** ~30,000 (expanded for World Cup)
- **Notable:** Canada's largest city brings diversity and energy. Toronto's multicultural population means every team has local supporters.

### BC Place — Vancouver
- **Capacity:** ~54,500
- **Notable:** The retractable roof handles Vancouver's unpredictable weather. Stunning mountain and ocean backdrop.

## What This Means for Fans

**Travel is the challenge.** Unlike a single-country World Cup, following your team could mean flights across an entire continent. The US alone spans 4 time zones. Planning ahead — flights, accommodation, and tickets — is essential.

**The atmosphere will be unmatched.** North America's immigrant communities mean every participating nation will have passionate local support. A Mexico match in Houston, a Colombian match in Miami, a Korean match in LA — these will feel like home games for the "visiting" team.

**Weather varies wildly.** June/July in Houston or Dallas means 35°C+ heat. Seattle or Vancouver could be a pleasant 20°C. Mexico City's altitude affects everyone. Player fitness and tactical adaptation will be tested like never before.
