# South Korea 🇰🇷 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** ~25
- **Confederation:** AFC
- **World Cup Appearances:** 11
- **Best Result:** Semi-finals (2002 — co-hosts)
- **Group:** A (vs Mexico, South Africa, UEFA Playoff D)
- **Key Question:** Can Son Heung-min lead one final World Cup charge?

## The Story
South Korea's 2002 semi-final run as co-hosts remains one of football's greatest fairy tales — beating Spain and Italy on the way. Since then, they've been consistent qualifiers but haven't recaptured that magic. Son Heung-min, one of Asia's greatest ever players, has carried this team on his shoulders for a decade.

2026 likely represents Son's last World Cup, and he'll want to go out with a statement. Group A alongside co-hosts Mexico is tough but navigable. South Korea's pressing intensity and never-die spirit make them a nightmare opponent for anyone.

## Playing Style
- Relentless high pressing — incredible fitness and work rate
- Son Heung-min as the focal point of every attack
- Quick transitions from defense to attack
- Disciplined defensive shape — hard to break down
- Emotional intensity — Korean fans create incredible energy

## Key Players to Watch
- **Son Heung-min** — Tottenham legend. One of the best attackers in Premier League history. Likely final World Cup.
- **Lee Kang-in** — PSG's creative talent. Technical brilliance and vision.
- **Kim Min-jae** — Bayern Munich's rock. One of Asia's best-ever defenders.
- **Hwang Hee-chan** — Wolves' striker. Pace and finishing in the Premier League.

## Strengths
✅ Son Heung-min is a genuine world-class difference maker
✅ Kim Min-jae provides elite defensive stability
✅ High pressing intensity exhausts opponents
✅ Consistent World Cup qualifiers — know the stage well
✅ 2002 semi-final pedigree — Korean football dreams big

## Weaknesses
❌ Son-dependent — if he's injured or marked out, quality drops significantly
❌ Mexico with home advantage in Group A is a tough draw
❌ Creative limitations beyond Son and Lee
❌ Can be exposed against physically dominant sides
❌ Haven't been past the group stage since 2010 (excluding 2002 as hosts)

## World Cup History Highlights
- 🥉 2002: SEMI-FINALISTS (co-hosts) — beat Spain, Italy. National heroes.
- 📊 2022: Round of 16 — beat Portugal 2-1 in dramatic group finale, lost to Brazil
- 📊 2010: Round of 16 — lost to Uruguay
- 😤 2018: Beat Germany 2-0 (eliminated the defending champions!)
