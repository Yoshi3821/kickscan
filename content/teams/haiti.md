# Haiti 🇭🇹 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** ~85
- **Confederation:** CONCACAF
- **World Cup Appearances:** 2 (first since 1974 — 52-year gap!)
- **Best Result:** Group stage (1974)
- **Group:** C (vs Brazil, Morocco, Scotland)

## The Story
Haiti's return to the World Cup after 52 years is one of the most emotional stories of 2026. The Caribbean nation — battered by earthquakes, political instability, and economic hardship — has produced a football team that defied the odds to qualify. The 1974 World Cup appearance (featuring the legendary Emmanuel Sanon, who scored against Italy's Dino Zoff and broke his unbeaten record) is the stuff of legend.

Group C is brutally tough — Brazil and Morocco are elite, Scotland are solid — but Haiti aren't here for results alone. They're here to represent a nation's resilience and prove Caribbean football belongs on the biggest stage.

## Playing Style
- Caribbean flair — skillful, creative, joyful football
- Physical and athletic — speed and power
- Counter-attacking through individual brilliance
- Emotional intensity — every game is a final for this team
- Improving tactical organization through CONCACAF experience

## Key Players to Watch
- MLS and CONCACAF-based talent forming the squad backbone
- Athletic forwards with pace and directness
- Players with dual-nationality eligibility from the Haitian diaspora

## Strengths
✅ 52-year return — the emotion and motivation are off the charts
✅ Caribbean athletic ability — fast and powerful
✅ 1974 legacy — Sanon's goal against Italy inspires a nation
✅ Massive diaspora in North America — incredible home support
✅ Nothing to lose — every moment is a celebration

## Weaknesses
❌ Brazil and Morocco are vastly superior
❌ Biggest quality gap of any team in their group
❌ Limited top-league exposure for most players
❌ 52-year World Cup absence — zero institutional experience
❌ Scotland are also stronger on paper for the 3rd-place battle
