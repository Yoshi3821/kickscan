# Panama 🇵🇦 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 30
- **Confederation:** CONCACAF
- **World Cup Appearances:** 2 (debut 2018)
- **Best Result:** Group stage (2018)
- **Group:** L (vs England, Croatia, Ghana)

## The Story
Panama's 2018 World Cup qualification was one of CONCACAF's greatest moments — the entire country shut down to celebrate. The tournament itself was rough (3 losses, 11 goals conceded), but scoring their first-ever World Cup goal against England was a moment of pure joy.

Now they're back, having won their CONCACAF qualifying group. The squad is stronger, more experienced, and better prepared. Group L is tough — England and Croatia are heavyweights — but Panama are no longer wide-eyed debutants. They've been here before.

## Playing Style
- CONCACAF physical intensity — aggressive, combative, hard-nosed
- Organized defensive shape — sit deep and frustrate
- Counter-attacking through direct play and set pieces
- Experienced in hostile CONCACAF qualifying environments
- Team spirit and national pride fuel everything

## Key Players to Watch
- Improved pool of MLS and European-based talent since 2018
- Physical midfielders who win aerial battles
- Experienced defensive spine

## Strengths
✅ Won CONCACAF qualifying group — genuine improvement since 2018
✅ 2018 World Cup experience — no longer naive debutants
✅ CONCACAF physicality — difficult to play against
✅ Passionate fan base — Panama stops when the national team plays
✅ Nothing to lose — pure underdog energy

## Weaknesses

❌ 2018 record (0W, 11 goals conceded) shows the quality gap
❌ Limited technical quality against top European sides
❌ Squad depth is thin
❌ Goal-scoring remains a major challenge against organized defenses
