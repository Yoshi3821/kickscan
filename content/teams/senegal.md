# Senegal 🇸🇳 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 19
- **Confederation:** CAF
- **World Cup Appearances:** 4
- **Best Result:** Quarter-finals (2002)
- **Group:** I (vs France, IC Playoff 2 winner, Norway)
- **Key Question:** Can the post-Mané generation replicate Africa's best World Cup run?

## The Story
Senegal are Africa's standard-bearers. AFCON 2022 champions, consistent World Cup qualifiers, and home to a deep pool of European-based talent. The 2002 World Cup — where they beat France in the opener and reached the quarter-finals — remains one of Africa's greatest football achievements. This generation wants to match it.

The challenge? Group I features France, the tournament's co-favorites. But Senegal have history against Les Bleus (beat them 1-0 in 2002's opening game) and the quality to compete. 2nd place is the realistic target, with Norway the main competition for qualification.

## Playing Style
- Athletic, fast, physically dominant — impossible to outrun
- Organized defensive structure with quick counter-attacks
- Technical quality across midfield — Ligue 1 and Premier League talent
- Set-piece threat — aerial dominance from corners
- AFCON-winning mentality — know how to win knockout football

## Key Players to Watch
- **Ismaïla Sarr** — Explosive winger with frightening pace. Nightmare for full-backs.
- **Abdoulaye Doucoure / Nampalys Mendy** — Midfield steel and experience
- **Kalidou Koulibaly** — If still active, defensive leadership and command
- **Nicolas Jackson** — Chelsea's striker. Goals and physical presence.
- **Pape Matar Sarr** — Tottenham's dynamic young midfielder. Box-to-box energy.

## Strengths
✅ AFCON 2022 champions — winning mentality
✅ Deep squad of European-based professionals
✅ Physical and athletic superiority against most opponents
✅ 2002 quarter-final pedigree — know how to perform at World Cups
✅ French football connections — many players know Ligue 1 opponents intimately

## Weaknesses
❌ Post-Mané era — losing their talisman leaves a creative void
❌ France in the group is a massive challenge
❌ Goalkeeping depth can be inconsistent
❌ Over-reliance on defensive solidity — can struggle to create consistently
❌ Africa's World Cup QF barrier — only 3 African teams have ever reached it

## World Cup History Highlights
- 🏆 2002: Quarter-finals — beat France 1-0 in opener, beat Sweden in R16
- 📊 2022: Round of 16 — lost to England 3-0
- 📊 2018: Group stage exit on fair play tiebreaker — cruel elimination
- 🏆 AFCON 2022: Champions — beat Egypt on penalties in the final
