# Cape Verde 🇨🇻 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 68
- **Confederation:** CAF
- **World Cup Appearances:** 1 (DEBUT!)
- **Best Result:** N/A — first ever World Cup
- **Group:** H (vs Spain, Saudi Arabia, Uruguay)

## The Story
Cape Verde at a World Cup is one of the greatest achievements in African football. An archipelago of ~600,000 people in the Atlantic Ocean — smaller than most cities — qualifying for football's biggest stage is nothing short of miraculous. They topped their CAF qualifying group to earn their place.

The island nation has punched above its weight for years — AFCON quarter-finalists in 2013 and 2023 — but this is a different level entirely. Group H features world #1 Spain and two-time champions Uruguay. It's a brutal draw, but Cape Verde aren't here by accident.

## Playing Style
- Portuguese-influenced technical football — connection to former colonial power
- Physical and athletic — island athletes with pace and power
- Organized defensive shape — disciplined and hard-working
- Counter-attacking through individual skill
- Team spirit above all — the entire nation is behind 11 players

## Key Players to Watch
- Players from Portuguese Primeira Liga and lower European leagues
- Athletic forwards with pace
- Experienced defenders from African and European competitions

## Strengths
✅ Historic debut — motivation and adrenaline off the charts
✅ AFCON quarter-final experience (2013, 2023) — not total unknowns
✅ Portuguese football connections provide tactical and technical quality
✅ 600,000 people with one dream — ultimate underdog energy
✅ Topped CAF qualifying group — earned it on merit

## Weaknesses
❌ Spain (#1) and Uruguay (2x champions) in the group — doesn't get harder
❌ Smallest nation at the World Cup alongside Curaçao
❌ No World Cup experience — everything is new
❌ Squad depth is extremely limited
❌ Quality gap is enormous against top-tier opposition
