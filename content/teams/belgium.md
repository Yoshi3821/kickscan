# Belgium 🇧🇪 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 8
- **Confederation:** UEFA
- **World Cup Appearances:** 15
- **Best Result:** Third place (2018)
- **Group:** G (vs Egypt, Iran, New Zealand)
- **Key Question:** Is the golden generation's window finally closing?

## The Story
Belgium's golden generation has been one of football's great "what ifs." Ranked #1 in the world for years, loaded with Hazard, De Bruyne, Lukaku, and Courtois — yet never won a major trophy. Third place at Russia 2018 was the peak, and Qatar 2022 was a disaster: group-stage exit after losing to Morocco and drawing with Croatia.

For 2026, Belgium face an identity crisis. The golden generation is aging (De Bruyne is 34-35), and the next wave needs to step up. Doku, Openda, Trossard bring energy and pace, but the midfield creativity that De Bruyne provided is irreplaceable. Group G (Egypt, Iran, New Zealand) is manageable — but Belgium's floor has dropped.

## Playing Style
- Transition-based attack with pace on the wings
- De Bruyne's creativity (if fit) unlocks defenses
- Physical and aerial threat from set pieces
- Courtois in goal provides world-class shot-stopping
- Evolving from golden-generation reliance to a more collective approach

## Key Players to Watch
- **Kevin De Bruyne** — Man City legend, likely final World Cup. Still world-class when fit.
- **Jérémy Doku** — Man City's explosive dribbler. The future.
- **Loïs Openda** — RB Leipzig's electric striker. Pace and finishing.
- **Thibaut Courtois** — Real Madrid's wall. One of the best keepers in World Cup history.
- **Leandro Trossard** — Arsenal's versatile attacker. Goals and assists.

## Strengths
✅ Still loaded with quality — De Bruyne, Courtois, Doku, Openda
✅ Group G is very favorable — should qualify comfortably
✅ 2018 third place provides genuine World Cup pedigree
✅ Courtois is a tournament-level difference maker
✅ Young attackers (Doku, Openda) bring pace and directness

## Weaknesses
❌ Golden generation aging — De Bruyne's fitness is a constant concern
❌ 2022 group-stage exit shattered confidence
❌ No major trophy despite years of being ranked #1 — the mental block is real
❌ Defensive vulnerabilities without Kompany-era leadership
❌ Chemistry concerns — transition between generations creates uncertainty

## World Cup History Highlights

- 😤 2022: Group-stage exit — lost to Morocco, drew Croatia. Humiliating.
- 📊 2014: Quarter-finalists — lost to Argentina
- 📊 1986: Semi-finalists — lost to Argentina (Maradona)
