# Jordan 🇯🇴 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 66
- **Confederation:** AFC
- **World Cup Appearances:** 1 (DEBUT!)
- **Best Result:** N/A — first ever World Cup

## The Story
Jordan's qualification is a landmark moment for Middle Eastern football. After decades of near-misses, the Nashama ("the brave ones") finally break through to the World Cup — fueled by their stunning 2023 Asian Cup run where they reached the final, losing to Qatar.

That Asian Cup campaign proved Jordan belong among Asia's elite. Now they face the ultimate test: defending champions Argentina headline Group J, with Austria (Rangnick's pressing machine) and Algeria (AFCON champions) also in the mix. It's a baptism of fire, but Jordan have nothing to lose and everything to prove.

## Playing Style
- Organized and disciplined — Middle Eastern tactical intelligence
- Defensive solidity built around the Asian Cup final run
- Counter-attacking through pace and directness
- Set-piece quality — well-drilled from dead balls
- Incredible team spirit and togetherness

## Key Players to Watch
- Asian Cup 2023 heroes forming the squad backbone
- Players from Gulf leagues and emerging European talent
- Experienced captain providing leadership

## Strengths
✅ 2023 Asian Cup finalists — proved they can compete at the highest Asian level
✅ Historic first World Cup — maximum motivation
✅ Organized defensive structure — hard to break down
✅ Nothing to lose — pure underdog energy
✅ Growing football infrastructure in Jordan

## Weaknesses
❌ Argentina, Austria, and Algeria are all significantly stronger
❌ No World Cup experience — everything is new
❌ Limited squad depth and European-league exposure
❌ Quality gap in individual talent vs group opponents
❌ Asian Cup final is their ceiling so far — World Cup is a step above
