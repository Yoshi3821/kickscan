# Egypt 🇪🇬 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 34
- **Confederation:** CAF
- **World Cup Appearances:** 4 (first since 2018)
- **Best Result:** Group stage
- **Group:** G (vs Belgium, Iran, New Zealand)
- **Key Question:** Can Mo Salah carry Egypt past the group stage for the first time?

## The Story
Egypt's football identity revolves around two things: 7 AFCON titles (Africa's most successful nation) and Mo Salah (one of the greatest players of his generation). Yet at World Cups, Egypt have been disappointing — their 2018 return after a 28-year absence ended with 3 losses in 3 games.

Group G offers redemption. Belgium are beatable, Iran are defensive but limited, and New Zealand are the group's weakest team. If Salah is fit and firing, Egypt have the quality to qualify for the knockout stage for the first time in their history.

## Playing Style
- Everything flows through Mo Salah — he IS the attack
- Organized defensive shape with quick counter-attacks
- Physical and aerial presence — North African intensity
- Set-piece quality — Salah's delivery and tall center-backs create danger
- AFCON-bred tournament mentality

## Key Players to Watch
- **Mohamed Salah** — Liverpool's king. Premier League legend, African football icon. One of the most complete forwards in history.
- **Mohamed Elneny** — Veteran midfielder, experienced and reliable
- **Omar Marmoush** — Emerging attacking talent from Bundesliga
- **Ahmed Hegazi** — Defensive leader, aerial dominance

## Strengths
✅ Mo Salah — a top-5 player in the world on his day
✅ 7x AFCON champions — Africa's most successful national team
✅ Organized defensive structure
✅ Massive fan base — Egyptian diaspora in North America will be loud
✅ Group G is favorable — realistic chance to qualify

## Weaknesses
❌ Salah-dependent — without him, quality drops dramatically
❌ 0 World Cup knockout appearances in history — psychological barrier
❌ 2018 World Cup was a disaster (3 losses, Salah injured)
❌ Creative limitations beyond Salah — who else creates?
❌ Aging squad depth behind the starting XI
