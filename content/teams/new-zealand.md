# New Zealand 🇳🇿 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** ~95
- **Confederation:** OFC
- **World Cup Appearances:** 3 (first since 2010)
- **Best Result:** Group stage (2010 — unbeaten! 3 draws including vs Italy)
- **Group:** G (vs Belgium, Egypt, Iran)

## The Story
New Zealand's 2010 World Cup is one of football's great stories. The All Whites went unbeaten — drawing with Italy (the defending champions), Slovakia, and Paraguay. They were the ONLY unbeaten team at that tournament. No wins, but no losses — and a legacy that still inspires Kiwi football.

Returning after 16 years, New Zealand face a realistic group in Belgium, Egypt, and Iran. They're heavy underdogs in every match, but the 2010 squad proved that spirit, organization, and a bit of Kiwi magic can produce remarkable results.

## Playing Style
- Organized, disciplined, hard to break down
- Physical and aerial — one of the tallest squads at the tournament
- Direct play through target man Chris Wood (if selected)
- Counter-attacking with efficiency — don't need possession to compete
- Incredible work rate — 11 men behind the ball when needed

## Key Players to Watch
- **Chris Wood** — Premier League striker, aerial dominance, proven goalscorer
- Emerging talent from A-League and lower European leagues
- Experienced defensive core

## Strengths
✅ 2010 unbeaten record — the ultimate underdog story
✅ Chris Wood gives them a genuine focal point
✅ Physical and aerial presence — tough to play against
✅ OFC dominance builds confidence
✅ Nothing to lose — pure underdog energy

## Weaknesses
❌ Massive quality gap vs Belgium, Egypt, and Iran
❌ OFC qualifying doesn't prepare for World Cup intensity
❌ Limited squad depth — very few top-league players
❌ Creative limitations — struggle to create chances
❌ Lowest-ranked team in the group by far

## 💰 Betting Outlook

- **New Zealand vs Iran — Under 2.5:** Both teams play tight. 1-0 or 0-0 territory.
