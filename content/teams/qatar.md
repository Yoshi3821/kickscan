# Qatar 🇶🇦 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** ~40
- **Confederation:** AFC
- **World Cup Appearances:** 2
- **Best Result:** Group stage (2022 — hosts)
- **Group:** B (vs Canada, Switzerland, UEFA Playoff A winner)

## The Story
Qatar's 2022 home World Cup was bittersweet — as hosts, they lost all three group games and became the first host nation eliminated in the group stage. Despite massive investment in football infrastructure and a 2019 Asian Cup title, the step up to World Cup level proved too much.

For 2026, Qatar return through merit — qualifying via the AFC pathway. Group B (Canada, Switzerland, UEFA Playoff A) is manageable, and the 2022 experience, painful as it was, provides lessons. The question is whether Qatar can translate Asian Cup quality to the World Cup stage.

## Playing Style
- Possession-based football developed through the Aspire Academy system
- Quick, technical, Spanish-influenced passing
- Organized defensive shape
- Counter-attacking through speed on the wings
- Set-piece routines are well-drilled

## Key Players to Watch
- **Akram Afif** — Asian Player of the Year. Creativity and goals.
- **Almoez Ali** — Prolific striker, Asian Cup hero
- Aspire Academy graduates forming the squad backbone

## Strengths
✅ 2019 Asian Cup champions — regional quality proven
✅ 2022 World Cup experience — learned hard lessons
✅ Aspire Academy produces technically gifted players
✅ Cohesive squad — most players have played together for years
✅ Group B is not as tough as 2022

## Weaknesses
❌ 2022 World Cup: 3 losses, 1 goal scored, 7 conceded — worst host record ever
❌ Domestic league doesn't prepare players for World Cup intensity
❌ Physical mismatch against European sides
❌ Limited international exposure for most players
❌ Switzerland and Canada are both stronger on paper
