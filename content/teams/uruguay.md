# Uruguay 🇺🇾 — Team Profile

## Quick Facts
- **FIFA Ranking:** 16
- **Confederation:** CONMEBOL
- **World Cup Appearances:** 15
- **Best Result:** Winners (1930, 1950)
- **Group:** H (vs Spain, Cape Verde, Saudi Arabia)
- **Key Question:** Can the post-Suárez/Cavani generation keep punching above their weight?

## The Story
Uruguay are football's original overachievers. A nation of 3.5 million people with 2 World Cup titles, 15 Copa Américas, and a reputation for producing warriors. The Suárez-Cavani-Godín era defined a golden generation that reached the 2010 semi-finals and consistently competed with South America's giants. Now the torch passes to Valverde, Núñez, Araújo — and the DNA of La Celeste demands they deliver.

The group draw is tough: Spain are the world's #1 ranked team. But Uruguay have faced tougher situations before. Saudi Arabia and Cape Verde are beatable, and a trademark 1-0 grind against Spain isn't out of the question. This is a team built to fight.

## Playing Style
- CONMEBOL grit — physical, aggressive, never give an inch
- Organized defensive structure — hard to break down
- Counter-attacking threat through Valverde's box-to-box runs
- Núñez brings chaos and pace to the front line
- Set pieces, dark arts, time-wasting — Uruguay use every tool available

## Key Players to Watch
- **Federico Valverde** — Real Madrid's ultimate midfielder. Power, pace, goals, and engine.
- **Darwin Núñez** — Liverpool's unpredictable striker. On fire or invisible — no in between.
- **Ronald Araújo** — Barcelona's defensive warrior. Physical, commanding, dominant in the air.
- **Facundo Pellistri** — Young winger with pace and directness
- **Nicolás De La Cruz** — Creative midfielder, set-piece specialist

## Strengths
✅ 2x World Cup champions — the pedigree is real
✅ Valverde is one of the best midfielders in world football
✅ CONMEBOL qualifying toughness — finished 4th in the world's hardest qualifiers
✅ Defensively elite — Uruguay concede very few goals
✅ Mentality — never beaten until the final whistle. Garra Charrúa is real.

## Weaknesses
❌ Post-golden generation transition — Suárez, Cavani, Godín are gone
❌ Squad depth is limited beyond the starting XI
❌ Núñez's inconsistency — world-class one game, anonymous the next
❌ Spain in the group is a huge challenge
❌ Creative limitations — can struggle to break down deep blocks

## World Cup History Highlights
- 🏆 1930: First ever World Cup champions (hosts)
- 🏆 1950: Maracanazo — beat hosts Brazil in the final. Football's greatest upset.
- 🥉 2010: Semi-finalists — Forlán's incredible tournament, Suárez handball vs Ghana
- 📊 2018: Quarter-finalists — beat Portugal, lost to France
- 😤 2022: Group-stage exit — dramatic final matchday, missed out on goal difference
